Places to Visit in Corfu

Achilleion Palace

The Achilleion Palace is located in the picturesque village of Gastouri, around 10 miles south of Corfu Town. The Palace was built in 1890 for Elizabeth, the former empress of Austria.

The Palace has immaculately kept gardens and has many sculpted statues which adorn it.
Elizabeth was facinated with Greek mythology and dedicated the Palace to Achilles including a striking statue of him located in the gardens.

Visitors can enter the Palace and walk through the gardens taking in the breathtaking beauty and great views over Corfu Island.

Corfu Old Town

The Corfu Old Town was mainly built by the Venetians, however it has also taken influences from the French and British occupations to become a wonderful and unique place to walk around.

The unique streets have an lovely allure with many tourist shops, tavernas and coffee shops throughout to choose from. As a visitor you will never be far from some hidden gems waiting to be found.

Old Fortress

Corfu Town has two fortresses with the Old Fortress being the main tourist attraction. It was built in 15th century by the Venetians overlooking the bay and the main square of the old town.

The fortress has a rich history which includes surviving two Ottoman sieges, ensuring the Island never fell into Ottoman hands.

Visitors have a number of activities they can do inlcuding:
- Climbing to the top of the fortress and get a spectacular 360 degree view of Corfu Town and the Island.
- Visit a small museum which displays relics from throughout it's history.
- Sit and take in the view there is a friendly bar at the foot of the fortress.

Vlacherna Monastery

One of the most iconic sights in Corfu, the monastery sits out on it's very own island out to sea.

It's connected to the main Island via a jetty and visitors can head into the monastery to light a candle and visit the gift shop. Alternatively you can book a tour that includes a trip to the local Mouse Island.
