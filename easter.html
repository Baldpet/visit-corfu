Corfu at Easter

Corfu at Easter

Easter is the biggest event in the Orthodox church calender and the island of Corfu takes this to a new level.

In Corfu Town over the Easter period there are processions, marching bands, fireworks, candle festival and of course the world famous pottery smashing!

It's really is a wonderful celebration of Easter that should be experienced at least once in your lifetime.

Good Friday

Although there are events held throughout the week starting from Palm Sunday, the main Easter events begin from Good Friday.

The epitaph processions come out from every church in Corfu Town and intersect at the centre of town. These processions follow a specific route which is lit by candles and through the crowed streets of local and tourist onlookers.

The last of these processions starts at 10pm and is accompanied by the 3 main bands of Corfu Island, each of which are playing their specific song. The processions route passes through Liston which has it's lanterns coloured in purple as a sign of mourning. (pictured)

Great Saturday

The events of and rites of Holy Saturday are perhaps the best moments of Easter in Corfu.

There are processions in the morning with the main bands playing funeral march creating a sombre atmosphere.

However, the mood soon changes by 11am where the celebration of the First Easter takes place, the churches bells toll joyfully and in the center of town pottery is thrown of the balconies and from people packing the streets. After which the bands come marching through playing joyful songs in celebration.

Easter Sunday

Following on from Great Saturday the Resurrection service takes place just after midnight in the bandstand of the Upper Spianada Square. The Bishop will announce to the crowds "the Risen Christ" which is the signal for the start of the fireworks display.

It is tradition to have lit a candle whilst the Resurrection takes place and it is quite a sight to see the whole sqaure covered in candles.

The celebration continues for the rest of the day where there is a big feast with the centre-point usually a lamb cooked on a spit. There are also more porcessions celebrating the local icons and relics of the Patron Saints.

Important Dates

As Orthodox Easter uses a different calender to determine when Easter falls to traditional Christian Easter it is important to note the dates for the years to come which are listed below:

Palm Sunday 12 April 25 April 17 April
Good Friday 17 April 30 April 22 April
